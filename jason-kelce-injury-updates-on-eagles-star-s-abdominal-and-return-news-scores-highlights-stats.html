    Updates from Monday, Sept. 22

    Eagles Insider passed along comments from Chip Kelly on Jason Kelce's injury:

    Dave Spadaro @EaglesInsider

    Chip Kelly: Just saw Kelce, he's going to have sports hernia surgery, not sure how long he'll be out for.

    Earlier, Howard Eskin of Fox 29 TV and 94WIP Radio reported Kelce was expected to undergo surgery:

    Howard Eskin @howardeskin

    Starting to hear that @eagles C Jason Kelce likely to have surgery for some form of hernia. Could miss up to 2 months. Eag looking 4 player

    Jeff McLane of The Philadelphia Inquirer quickly reiterated:

    Jeff McLane @Jeff_McLane

    Initial tests are that Jason Kelce has a sports hernia and will have surgery, sources confirm @howardeskin tweet. Getting second opinion.

    Jeff McLane @Jeff_McLane

    Surgery is pretty much a definite for Jason Kelce, sources say. More tests will indicate time frame, but could be out for 6-8 weeks or more.

    Original Text

    The Philadelphia Eagles took a big hit in the third quarter of Sunday's contest against the Washington Redskins. Veteran center Jason Kelce left the game with an apparent abdominal injury, according to a tweet from Eagles Insider:

    Dave Spadaro @EaglesInsider

    Jason Kelce is out with an abdominal injury

    Kelce is arguably the team's most valuable offensive lineman, as its high-octane offense revolves around its athletic big men in the trenches. David Molk came in to replace the injured starter.
