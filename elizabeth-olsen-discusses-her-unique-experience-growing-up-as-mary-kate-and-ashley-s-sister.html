    Elizabeth Olsen discusses her 'unique' experience growing up asMary-Kate andAshley's sister

    Elizabeth Olsen discussed her unique experiencegrowing up as Mary-Kate and Ashleys sister on Thursdays episode of Lorraine. TheWandaVision star,31, joked that it wasnt an average childhood due to her twin siblings, 34, global fame.

    13 Jul 2024, 00:00

    Elizabeth Olsen discussed her ‘unique’ experience growing up as Mary-Kate and Ashley’s sister on Thursday’s episode of Lorraine.

    The WandaVision star, 31, joked that it wasn’t an ‘average’ childhood due to her twin siblings, 34, global fame.

    Mary-Kate and Ashley started their career aged nine-months-old when they were cast as Michelle Tanner in Full House, a role they pursued until 1995.

    ‘Unique’: Elizabeth Olsen discussed her ‘unique’ experience growing up as Mary-Kate and Ashley’s sister on Thursday’s episode of Lorraine

    The twin siblings went on to release their own films, books, merchandise and clothing range.   

    In 2007, Forbes ranked Mary-Kate and Ashley jointly as the 11th-richest women in entertainment, with an estimated combined net worth of $100 million. 

    Talking about growing up as Mary-Kate and Ashley’s younger sister, Elizabeth told host Lorraine Kelly: ‘It’s not anyone’s average childhood, but everyone’s childhood is not average. I guess it was unique to me and my sisters’ experience.’  

    Away from her siblings’ fame, Elizabeth has cemented her own successful acting career as she is currently promoting her new Disney+ series WandaVision.

    Talking about the new show, the star said: ‘It’s definitely different than what people are used to seeing on the Avengers.

    ‘We have these characters, they are these super heroes that are just trying to fit in the suburbs, but things aren’t what they seem and it will be revealed in every episode.’

    Elizabeth, who plays Wanda Maximoff (Scarlet Witch), stars in the series alongside Paul Bettany, who plays Vision. 

    Their characters enjoyed a brief and epic romance in the Marvel Cinematic Universe, they have reprised their roles in the new Disney+ series.

    Talking about working with Paul, 49, Elizabeth said: ‘I’ve had so much fun with working him. He became one of these great comedy characters, he fit into that role very easily.’

    The actress also teased new details about filming the Doctor Strange franchise, she said: ‘We’re hoping to start next week. I really do trust that our crew are taking good care of themselves and their family.’  

    Elizabeth also sweetly said that she loves living in London and that it is her ‘home from home’.

    She said: ‘We love it here, we’re so happy here, we love walking in the rain and bundling up. It’s so nice to quarantine everywhere else, even if it’s inside our home.

    ‘Every opportunity I have to come here, I seize it. Even if I’m in Europe, I figure out how to swing to London. It’s my home from home.’ 

    WandaVision will take place following the events of 2019’s Avengers: Endgame, but so far far it’s not clear how Vision, who was killed in Infinity War, was revived.

    It’s also been revealed that the show will feature characters from Captain Marvel, Thor and Ant-Man, and it will tie into the upcoming 2022 movie Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness. 

    The series, which has been long awaited by Marvel fans, was also one of the many productions shut down in the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic last year.
