    Kimberley Martin Biography

    Kimberley Martin is an American sports journalist working as an NFL reporter at ESPN. She is also a contributor for Fantasy Football Now, Sports Center, Outside the Lines, and NFL Live among others. Previously, she worked for two years as the senior NFL writer at Yahoo Sports. She also co-hosted the network’s podcast alongside Charly Arnolt and Elle Duncan.

    Mainly, she covers the league year-round and also contributes to the Network’s Fantasy Football Now. While she was at Yahoo, she conducted live interviews with league executives and players, co-hosted Cover 3 which was an all-female weekly football show, and also wrote columns. Additionally, she worked as a beat writer at The Washington Post. Moreover, she worked at The Buffalo News as a columnist and Newsday as a New York Jets beat writer.

    Furthermore, she co-hosts the network’s podcast called First Take where they discuss major stories in sports and culture and also provide insight into their professions and personalities. She attended Wesleyan University where she graduated with a bachelor of arts degree in Psychology and African-American Studies. Later, she joined Syracuse University where she graduated with a master’s degree in newspaper and online journalism.
