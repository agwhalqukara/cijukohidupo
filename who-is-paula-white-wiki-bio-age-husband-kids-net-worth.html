    Paula White Biography

    Paula White was born on April 20, 1966, in Tupelo, Mississippi. Her parents owned a toy and craft store in Tupelo. Paula’s childhood was not like the ones in books. When she was five years old, her parents divorced. That was exacerbated when her father committed suicide. Her family was forced into poverty as a result of this situation, and her mother succumbed to alcoholism.

    She was raised by caregivers at the time. In an interview, she stated that she had a terrible childhood because she was mentally and physically abused by numerous people. Paula moved to her stepfather’s house in Washington D.C. after her mother’s second marriage to a military man. Paula attended Seneca Valley High School in Maryland.

    Career

    She pledged to devote her life to God at the age of 18 and converted to Christianity at the Damascus Church of God. Her life was not going well before meeting Randy White, but it took a drastic turn around after that. Paula and Randy established the Without Walls International Church, formerly known as the South Tampa Christian Center. Their church’s early days were not as smooth as they had hoped. They didn’t have the reorganization or even the support of the local administration in the beginning.

    They didn’t even have a salary for the first two years because their financial situation had collapsed. The church was reorganized as a result of its numerous programs, all of which required a great deal of hard work and dedication. With 20,000 parishioners, the Without Walls International Church was named the seventh-largest church in the United States in 2014. Due to her husband’s failing health, Paula was named senior pastor of Without Walls International Church in 2009.

    Paula wanted to reach out to a larger audience, so she chose the popular medium of television. In 2001, she taped her first episode of “Paula White Today,” which aired on nine of the most popular television networks, including Trinity Broadcast Network and Black Entertainment Television. Following the demise of her show, she became a minister to a number of well-known people, including Darryl Strawberry, Michael Jackson, and Gary Sheffield. She has also been appointed as a personal minister to President Donald Trump of the United States.
